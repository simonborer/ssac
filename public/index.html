What is accessibility?

Accessibility is a recognition that different people face different social barriers, and that we have a responsibility to work together to remove those barriers wherever possible.

Web accessibility is about meeting standards and using specific techniques in design, content and development so that your website is accessible to people with a range of physical and cognitive challenges.

Laws enforcing web accessibility as a human right are in effect in most major markets, including Canada, the U.S., and the E.U.

Why be accessible?

The easiest answer is "it's the law".

But most people aren't aware of the benefits of making your web content accessible. The simple fact is that most of "being accessible" is simply a matter of making your website's code respect web standards. Can't I just install this plugin?

While there are some very good tools for auditing & enhancing your accessibility, a large number of accessibility techniques are meant to provide context for visually impaired users that isn't evident in the code or content. For a simple example, a plugin can tell you that an image needs alternative text, but it can't tell you what that alternative text should be.
